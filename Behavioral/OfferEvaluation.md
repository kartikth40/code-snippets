# Offer Evaluation Framework

## 🎯 Overview

Evaluating a job offer is more than comparing base salaries. This framework helps you make a holistic, data-driven decision — especially when comparing multiple offers.

---

## 📊 The 5-Dimension Framework

Score each dimension 1-5, then weight by your priorities.

### Dimension 1: Compensation (Weight: varies)

```
Total Comp = Base + Annual Bonus + RSU/ESOP (annualized) + Benefits

Calculate annualized RSU:
  ₹40L grant over 4 years = ₹10L/year (at current valuation)

Example comparison:
                    Offer A (FAANG)    Offer B (Startup)
Base:               ₹45L              ₹35L
Bonus (target):     ₹9L (20%)         ₹3.5L (10%)
RSU/ESOP/year:      ₹12L              ₹5L (discounted 80%)
Benefits:           ₹3L               ₹1L
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Total Comp/year:    ₹69L              ₹44.5L

Score: Rate 1-5 vs your target compensation
```

**Questions to ask:**
- What's the bonus payout history? (Target vs actual)
- What's the RSU refresh policy after year 4?
- Is there a joining bonus? (One-time, but helps)
- What's the salary review cycle?

---

### Dimension 2: Role & Impact (Weight: high for career growth)

```
Evaluate:
- Scope: How many users/systems will you impact?
- Ownership: Will you own features end-to-end or be a cog?
- Visibility: Will your work be seen by leadership?
- Influence: Can you shape technical direction?

Score 1-5:
1 = Narrow scope, no ownership, invisible work
5 = High impact, full ownership, visible to leadership
```

**Questions to ask:**
- What will I own in the first 6 months?
- How does this team's work connect to company priorities?
- What's the biggest project I'd work on?

---

### Dimension 3: Growth & Learning (Weight: high for early career)

```
Evaluate:
- Promotion timeline: How long to next level?
- Mentorship: Is there a strong senior engineer culture?
- Tech stack: Will you learn in-demand skills?
- Breadth: Can you move across teams/domains?
- L&D budget: Conferences, courses, certifications?

Score 1-5:
1 = No clear path, no mentorship, legacy tech
5 = Clear ladder, strong mentors, cutting-edge tech
```

**Red flags:**
- "We don't have a formal career ladder"
- High turnover at senior levels
- No internal mobility

---

### Dimension 4: Team & Culture (Weight: high for day-to-day happiness)

```
Evaluate:
- Manager quality: Is your manager a coach or a micromanager?
- Team dynamics: Collaborative or siloed?
- Engineering culture: High standards or "just ship it"?
- Work-life balance: Sustainable pace or constant crunch?
- Psychological safety: Can you disagree without fear?

Score 1-5:
1 = Toxic manager, no collaboration, constant crunch
5 = Great manager, strong team, sustainable pace
```

**How to assess:**
- Ask team members (not just the manager) during interviews
- Check Glassdoor reviews (look for patterns, not outliers)
- Ask: "How does the team handle failures?"
- Ask: "What's the on-call burden like?"

---

### Dimension 5: Company Trajectory (Weight: high for equity value)

```
Evaluate:
- Stage: Early startup vs growth vs public company
- Funding: Runway, last round, investors
- Revenue: Growing or declining?
- Market: Is the market growing?
- Mission: Do you believe in what they're building?

Score 1-5:
1 = Declining revenue, unclear path, weak market
5 = Strong growth, clear path to IPO/profitability, exciting market
```

**For startups specifically:**
- Series A: High risk, high reward (ESOPs most valuable if it works)
- Series B/C: Medium risk, medium reward
- Pre-IPO: Lower risk, still meaningful equity upside
- Public: Lowest risk, RSUs are liquid

```
                    Offer A    Offer B    Offer C
                    Weight     Score      Score      Score
Compensation        30%        4          3          5
Role & Impact       25%        5          4          3
Growth & Learning   20%        4          5          3
Team & Culture      15%        4          3          4
Company Trajectory  10%        3          5          2
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Weighted Score                 4.1        3.9        3.6
```

**Adjust weights based on your priorities:**
- Early career: Weight Growth & Learning higher (30%)
- Mid-career: Weight Role & Impact higher (30%)
- Financial goals: Weight Compensation higher (40%)
- Burned out: Weight Team & Culture higher (30%)

## 🔄 Comparing Multiple Offers

### The Deadline Problem

```
Scenario: Offer A expires in 3 days, but you're waiting for Offer B.

Options:
1. Ask Offer A for extension:
   "I'm very interested in this role. I have another process 
   wrapping up in [X days]. Could you extend the deadline to [date]?"
   Most companies will give 3-7 more days.

2. Accelerate Offer B:
   "I have a competing offer with a deadline of [date]. 
   I'm very interested in your company — is there any way 
   to expedite the process?"
   Many companies will fast-track if you're a strong candidate.

3. Make a decision with incomplete information:
   If Offer A is strong and Offer B is uncertain, 
   don't let perfect be the enemy of good.
```

### When Offers Are Close

```
If two offers score within 0.3 of each other:
- Go with your gut (which team excited you more?)
- Consider the manager (you work FOR a person, not a company)
- Consider the mission (what will you be proud to say you worked on?)
- Consider the commute/location (underrated quality-of-life factor)
```

## 🚩 Red Flags in Offers

```
Compensation red flags:
❌ Offer expires in <48 hours (pressure tactic)
❌ Vague equity ("we'll figure it out after you join")
❌ No written offer letter
❌ Bonus "at discretion" with no target %

Role red flags:
❌ Unclear responsibilities ("you'll figure it out")
❌ Reporting to someone who just joined
❌ Role created for you but no headcount approved

Culture red flags:
❌ Interviewer badmouths current/former employees
❌ Disorganized interview process (reflects company culture)
❌ No one on the team has been there >2 years
❌ "We work hard and play hard" (often means just work hard)

Company red flags:
❌ Declining to share revenue/growth metrics
❌ Recent layoffs with no explanation
❌ Founder/CEO has history of failed ventures with no learnings
❌ Pivoting business model frequently
```

---

## ✅ Green Flags

```
✅ Interviewers speak enthusiastically about their work
✅ Clear career ladder with examples of promotions
✅ Transparent about challenges ("here's what we're working on")
✅ Strong engineering blog / open source contributions
✅ Low turnover at senior levels
✅ Manager has been promoted from within
✅ Company growing revenue YoY
✅ Offer deadline is reasonable (1 week+)
```

## 📝 Decision Checklist

Before accepting, confirm:

**Compensation:**
- [ ] Total comp calculated (base + bonus + equity/year)
- [ ] Equity terms understood (vesting, cliff, strike price)
- [ ] Joining bonus confirmed in writing
- [ ] Salary review cycle confirmed

**Role:**
- [ ] Job description matches what was discussed
- [ ] Reporting structure confirmed
- [ ] Team size and composition known

**Logistics:**
- [ ] Start date agreed
- [ ] Notice period / buyout discussed
- [ ] Relocation support (if needed) confirmed
- [ ] Work location / WFH policy confirmed

**Gut check:**
- [ ] Am I excited to tell people I work here?
- [ ] Do I respect the people I'll work with?
- [ ] Does this move me toward my 3-year goal?
