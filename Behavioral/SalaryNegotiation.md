# Salary Negotiation Guide

## 🎯 Why Negotiation Matters

Most candidates leave money on the table by not negotiating. Studies show:
- **85%** of hiring managers have room to negotiate
- Average negotiation yields **10-20%** more compensation
- First offer is rarely the best offer — companies expect negotiation

**Key mindset:** Negotiation is professional, expected, and respected. It signals you know your worth.

## 🗣️ The Negotiation Playbook

### Rule 1: Never Give a Number First

```
Recruiter: "What are your salary expectations?"

❌ Bad: "I'm looking for ₹30 LPA"
(You've anchored low before knowing their range)

✅ Good: "I'm more interested in finding the right fit. 
Could you share the budgeted range for this role?"

OR if pressed:
"Based on my research and experience, I'm targeting 
market rate for this level. What's the range you have 
budgeted for this position?"
```

### Rule 2: Get the Full Offer in Writing Before Negotiating

```
Don't negotiate on a verbal offer.
Ask: "Could you send me the offer letter so I can review 
the complete compensation package?"

Review:
- Base salary
- Joining bonus
- Annual bonus (target %)
- RSUs/ESOPs (vesting schedule)
- Benefits (health, PF, gratuity)
- Notice period buyout (if applicable)
```

### Rule 3: Negotiate the Total Package, Not Just Base

```
Components to negotiate:
1. Base salary (most important, affects all future raises)
2. Joining/signing bonus (one-time, easier to give)
3. RSU/ESOP grant (negotiate number of units)
4. Performance bonus target %
5. Notice period buyout (if current employer requires notice)
6. Start date (more time = more leverage sometimes)
```

## 💬 Negotiation Scripts

### Initial Counter-Offer

```
Scenario: Received ₹28 LPA offer, want ₹35 LPA

Script:
"Thank you so much for the offer — I'm genuinely excited 
about the role and the team. After reviewing the package 
and researching market rates for this level, I was hoping 
we could get closer to ₹35 LPA. 

Based on my [X years of experience / specific skills / 
competing offers], I believe this reflects my market value. 
Is there flexibility to move in that direction?"

Key elements:
✅ Express genuine enthusiasm first
✅ Give a specific number (not a range)
✅ Justify with data (market research, experience, competing offers)
✅ Ask a question — don't make it a demand
```

### When They Say "This Is Our Best Offer"

```
Script:
"I appreciate you checking. I'm very interested in joining 
and want to make this work. If the base isn't flexible, 
could we explore other components — perhaps a higher 
joining bonus or an additional RSU grant? 

I want to make sure I'm joining at a number I feel good 
about long-term."

Why this works:
- Doesn't accept defeat immediately
- Pivots to other components
- Shows you're serious about joining
```

### Using a Competing Offer

```
Script:
"I want to be transparent — I do have another offer at 
₹38 LPA from [Company]. I'm genuinely more excited about 
this role and team, which is why I'm having this conversation. 
Is there any way to close the gap?"

Rules:
✅ Only use if you actually have the offer
✅ Don't bluff — it can backfire badly
✅ Mention the company if it's a credible competitor
✅ Emphasize you prefer THIS company (don't sound like you're just using them)
```

### Asking for Time

```
Script:
"Thank you for the offer. I'm very excited about this 
opportunity. Could I have until [specific date — 3-5 days] 
to review the complete package and get back to you?"

Most companies will give 3-7 days.
Use this time to:
- Research market rates
- Get competing offers if possible
- Consult mentors/peers
- Evaluate the full package
```

## 🧮 Evaluating RSUs/ESOPs

### RSU Vesting Schedule (MAANG)

```
Typical 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff:
Year 1: 25% vests (cliff)
Year 2: 25% vests (quarterly)
Year 3: 25% vests (quarterly)
Year 4: 25% vests (quarterly)

Example: ₹40L RSU grant
Year 1: ₹10L vests (if stock price unchanged)
Year 2: ₹10L vests
Year 3: ₹10L vests
Year 4: ₹10L vests

Annualized value: ₹10L/year
Total comp = Base + Bonus + ₹10L RSU/year
```

### ESOP Evaluation (Startups)

```
Key questions to ask:
1. How many options am I getting? (absolute number)
2. What's the total shares outstanding? (to calculate %)
3. What's the current valuation? (to estimate value)
4. What's the strike price? (what you pay to exercise)
5. What's the vesting schedule?
6. What happens to ESOPs if I leave before vesting?
7. Is there a liquidity event planned?

Formula:
ESOP value = (Current valuation × your %) - (strike price × shares)

Example:
- 10,000 options at ₹10 strike price
- Company valued at ₹500Cr, 1Cr total shares
- Your % = 10,000 / 1,00,00,000 = 0.01%
- Value = (₹500Cr × 0.01%) - (10,000 × ₹10) = ₹5L - ₹1L = ₹4L

Reality check: Startup ESOPs are illiquid and risky.
Discount by 70-90% for realistic value.
```

## ⚠️ Common Negotiation Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Accepting first offer immediately | Leaves money on table | Always counter at least once |
| Giving a range | They'll pick the low end | Give a specific number |
| Negotiating via email only | Easier to say no in writing | Call or video for key conversations |
| Burning bridges if rejected | You may work with them later | Always stay professional |
| Lying about competing offers | Can be verified, destroys trust | Only use real offers |
| Negotiating after accepting | Unprofessional | Negotiate before accepting |
| Focusing only on base | Missing total comp picture | Evaluate full package |

## 📋 Offer Evaluation Checklist

Before accepting any offer, evaluate:

**Compensation:**
- [ ] Base salary (market rate?)
- [ ] Joining bonus (one-time)
- [ ] Annual bonus (target % and history of payout)
- [ ] RSU/ESOP grant (vesting schedule, current value)
- [ ] Total comp = Base + Bonus + RSU/year

**Benefits:**
- [ ] Health insurance (self + family coverage)
- [ ] PF/Gratuity contributions
- [ ] Meal/transport allowance
- [ ] Learning & development budget
- [ ] Work from home policy

**Role & Growth:**
- [ ] Scope of role (team size, ownership)
- [ ] Growth trajectory (promotion timeline)
- [ ] Manager quality (research on LinkedIn)
- [ ] Team culture (Glassdoor reviews)

**Logistics:**
- [ ] Notice period (current employer)
- [ ] Relocation support (if applicable)
- [ ] Start date flexibility

## 🎯 Post-Negotiation

### If They Meet Your Ask
```
"Thank you so much — I'm thrilled to accept. 
I'm excited to join the team and contribute from day one."
```

### If They Can't Move
```
"I appreciate you going to bat for me. 
I understand the constraints. I'd like to accept — 
could we revisit compensation at my 6-month review 
based on performance?"

(Get this in writing if they agree)
```

### If You Decline
```
"Thank you for the offer and for your time throughout 
the process. After careful consideration, I've decided 
to pursue another opportunity that's a better fit for 
my goals right now. I hope our paths cross again."

Always leave the door open — the tech world is small.
```
