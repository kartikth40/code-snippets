## ü§ù Referral Strategies (Step-by-Step)

### **Strategy 1: LinkedIn Networking (Highest Success Rate)**

**Step 1: Find the Right People**
```
LinkedIn Search:
"Software Engineer at [Company] [City]"

Filters:
- 2nd connections (mutual friends increase response)
- Same college (IIT, NIT, BITS alumni help each other)
- Same previous company
- People who post actively (more likely to respond)
```

**Step 2: Send Personalized Connection Request**
```
‚ùå Bad (Generic):
"Hi, please connect"

‚úÖ Good (Personalized):
"Hi [Name], I see we both studied at [College] and worked at [Company]. 
I'm exploring opportunities at Google Bangalore and would love to learn 
about your experience in the [Team] team. Would you be open to connecting?"
```

**Response Rate:** 40-60% for same college/company

---

**Step 3: Follow Up After Connection**
```
‚úÖ Template (24 hours after connecting):
"Thanks for connecting, [Name]! I've been preparing for Google SDE-2 
roles (solved 200+ LeetCode, reviewed system design) and am particularly 
interested in [Specific Team like Google Pay/YouTube].

Would you have 15 minutes for a quick chat about your journey and the 
team culture? Happy to work around your schedule.

Resume: [Google Drive link - make sure it's accessible]"
```

**Response Rate:** 30-50%

---

**Step 4: The Call (15-20 minutes)**
```
Questions to Ask:
1. "What team are you on, and what do you build?"
2. "What's unique about working in Google Bangalore vs other offices?"
3. "What do you wish you knew before joining?"
4. "How's the work-life balance?"
5. (At the end) "I'd love to apply for [Specific role]. Would you be 
   comfortable providing a referral?"

DO:
‚úÖ Be genuinely curious (not transactional)
‚úÖ Take notes, reference them later
‚úÖ Thank them for their time

DON'T:
‚ùå Ask for referral in first message
‚ùå Make call all about you
‚ùå Forget to follow up with thank you
```

**Referral Rate:** 70% if conversation goes well

---

**Step 5: After Referral**
```
‚úÖ Send thank you message:
"Thank you so much for the referral! I really appreciate you taking 
the time to chat and support my application. I'll keep you posted on 
the process."

‚úÖ Update them on progress:
- Phone screen scheduled
- Onsite scheduled  
- Offer received (thank them again!)

This maintains relationship for future (and they may help others you refer).
```

## üìä Salary Negotiation (India-Specific Tips)

### **Rule 1: Always Have Competing Offers**

**The Multiplier Effect:**
```
No competing offer: ‚Çπ40L offer
With competing offer: ‚Çπ40L ‚Üí ‚Çπ48L (20% increase!)
```

**How to Create Competition:**
```
1. Apply to multiple companies simultaneously (Google, Meta, Amazon, 
   Flipkart, Razorpay)
2. Time your interviews to get offers within 2 weeks of each other
3. Be transparent: "I'm in final rounds with [Company X], expecting 
   offer by [Date]"
```

---

### **Rule 2: Research Salary Ranges**

**Resources for India:**
- **levels.fyi/India** - Most accurate FAANG data
- **Glassdoor India** - Good for startups
- **AmbitionBox** - Indian company reviews + salaries
- **Blind India** - Anonymous discussions (use with skepticism)
- **Reddit r/developersIndia** - Salary threads

**What to Look For:**
- Base salary range for your level
- Stock component (RSUs vs ESOPs)
- Joining bonus
- Location differential (Bangalore vs Hyderabad)

---

### **Rule 3: Negotiate Beyond Base**

**Components to Negotiate:**

| Component | Negotiability | Strategy |
|-----------|---------------|----------|
| **Base Salary** | Medium | Use competing offer: "Company X offered ‚Çπ50L base" |
| **Signing Bonus** | High | "Can you increase signing to ‚Çπ10L to offset leaving bonus?" |
| **Stock (RSUs)** | Medium-High | "Can we increase equity to ‚Çπ40L/year?" |
| **Relocation** | High | "I'm moving from [City], can you cover ‚Çπ2L relocation?" |
| **Remote Flexibility** | Medium | "Can I work remote 2 days/week?" |
| **Learning Budget** | High | "Can you add ‚Çπ50K/year for courses/conferences?" |

**Example Negotiation:**
```
Initial Offer:
- Base: ‚Çπ45L
- Stock: ‚Çπ30L/year (4-year vest)
- Signing: ‚Çπ5L
- Total Year 1: ‚Çπ57.5L

After Negotiation:
- Base: ‚Çπ48L (+‚Çπ3L - "Company X offered ‚Çπ50L")
- Stock: ‚Çπ35L/year (+‚Çπ5L - "This brings total comp closer to my expectations")
- Signing: ‚Çπ10L (+‚Çπ5L - "Leaving ‚Çπ3L bonus at current company + relocation")
- Remote: 2 days/week (WFH saves ‚Çπ5K/month commute)
- Total Year 1: ‚Çπ66.75L

Result: ‚Çπ9.25L increase (16%)!
```

### **Rule 5: Know When to Stop**

**Red Flags You're Pushing Too Hard:**
- Recruiter says "This is our final offer" twice
- They start questioning your interest in the role
- They mention "band limits" (real constraint in big companies)

**When to Accept:**
- Offer is within 10% of your target
- Better than all other offers
- You're excited about the role (culture, team, growth)

**Remember:** Don't lose a great opportunity over ‚Çπ2-3L

- Interoperability requirements

**IT Act & Data Protection:**

**Why This Matters:**
Shows you understand constraints of building in India (not just copying US systems).
